Test Structure (2025 Update)

The ACT can now be taken digitally or on paper in the United States. It includes four core sections: English, Math, Reading, and Science. The Writing section is optional. The total testing time is about 2 hours and 55 minutes, not including breaks or writing.

Sections and Timing

  • English: 45 minutes, 75 questions
  • Math: 60 minutes, 60 questions
  • Reading: 35 minutes, 40 questions
  • Science: 35 minutes, 40 questions
  • Writing (optional): 40 minutes, 1 essay

How to Study

Start by taking a full-length practice test to identify your weak areas. After each test, review your mistakes and learn why you missed each question. Create a balanced study plan and focus on both accuracy and timing. Make use of official ACT practice tools and high-quality free resources listed below.

Recent Changes to the ACT

Starting in 2025, ACT testing centers began expanding digital test options. The digital version offers new features like on-screen highlighting, built-in timers, and answer flagging. Content and scoring remain the same as the paper version. Before test day, log in to your MyACT account to check which format your test center uses.
